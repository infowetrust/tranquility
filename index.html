Neil and Buzz Go for a Walk

By RJ ANDREWS. JULY 2019


After four days of travel and a daring landing, Apollo 11’s Lunar Module Eagle touched down on the Moon. Six hours later the hatch opened.  NEIL ARMSTRONG  and  BUZZ ALDRIN  emerged. They spent the following two and a half hours exploring an area the size of a baseball diamond.

This is the story—told through highlights from the original transmission log, illustrated photographs and maps, and a timeline at the bottom of your screen—about what happened on their Moonwalk.

By the end of their walk, Neil and Buzz collected 48 pounds of rocks and soil, made geologic observations, deployed experiments, shot photographs, received a phonecall from President Nixon, and inspected the condition of the spacecraft that would soon blast them back to Michael Collins.

But first Neil & Buzz needed rest. They stayed on the moon for one night to sleep. Neil slung a makeshift harness and Buzz curled up beneath him on the floor. With glaring lights and cold suits, only Buzz was able to doze off for a little bit.
